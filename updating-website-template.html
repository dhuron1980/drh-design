Updating Website Template

What questions should a graduate program website answer?

Group Research | Personas | Transform Findings

Instead of copying and pasting our website content into a new template, I decided to optimize the content for potential graduate students. To do this right, though, I needed to know what types of information these students needed and when they needed it.

Original Counseling Psychology program homepage.

Research

I began by researching the decision-making process with a focus on choosing college programs. Using student profiles posted on our website, I developed user personas for our Counseling Psychology and School Psychology programs. This information helped develop questions for the next round of research.

Counseling Psychology student profile page.

Profile data broken down into a spreadsheet.

Two personas: Angie, a future School Psychology student, and Jada, a future Counseling Psychology student.

I intended to interview undergraduate students about their graduate school search process. Because I worked alone, I decided this approach would take too much time. Instead, I organized group journey mapping sessions with current graduate students and program faculty. The conversations and maps created during these sessions helped me see the search process from multiple perspectives.

Data from the group journey mapping session.

I used my findings to develop a map outlining the questions students might ask during the college search process.

Transform Findings

My research showed that potential students visited our website during the early stages of their search. Our old site was hosting information students needed during the latter stages of their search or after they began the program. For the new site, I prioritized general program information, financial aid, research activities and information about the City of Houston. I am working to ensure program website copy reflects the unique motivations and qualities of their target student.

The updated Counseling Psychology website.

Future Projects

This project was motivated by the need to update our template. In the future, I will user test the template to determine if it meets the needs of our potential students. I also need more feedback from undergraduate students. One benefit of working for one department is the ability to design research projects that build on the findings of previous projects. With that in mind, my next effort will focus heavily on undergrads.
